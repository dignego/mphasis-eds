Archaic applications limit the ability to innovate and pose the following challenges:</p><ul>
<li>Monolithic design – Existing core systems are designed to tightly couple data, workflows and embedded rules. This makes them inflexible, creates a need for system wide regression testing upon any changes, and does not support new business process models that leverage the latest technological advancements</li>
<li>Lack of knowledge – Work done on applications by multiple subject matter experts over many years is lost due to attrition or workforce aging. The business logic, data flows and dependencies cannot be easily extracted from code or older documentation</li>
<li>Complex integration – Legacy monolithic applications have complex integrations between modules, requiring complex testing and production implementation effort, and delayed time-to-market</li>
<li>High cost of maintenance – Cost of maintaining older applications becomes prohibitive, mainly due to shrinking expert resource pools</li>
